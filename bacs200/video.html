What is Wild Tomorrow Fund?

Location: The Wild Tomorrow Fund is a non-profit organization primarily dedicated to protect threatened and endangered species. They are currently located in Southern Africa.

Their Focus: John Steward along with a few others founded the Wild Tomorrow Fund. They began by setting up the fund to supply conservation managers and rangers with equipment needed to keep wild life safe. As time passed, their objective expanded to include the protection of threatened and endangered species and their habitats.

Their main focus now it to save animals such as elephants, rhinos, lions and giraffes by providing in-field conservation. By providing in-field conservation for these wild animals, it keeps them safe from poachers. Wild Tomorrow Fund also rehabilitates wild animals that have suffered an injury and releases them back into the wild when they are ready. The foundation also works hard to conserve and protect land in South Africa so that the animals habitat aren’t wiped out by humans.

How you can be a part of their mission:

Volunteer The Wild Tomorrow fund allows you to sign up for group sessions or individual sessions to volunteer. While volunteering they actually take the time to educate you on the life of many wild animals. They teach you about the importance of the animals habitats and surrounding communities. Volunteers get to see wild animals in their natural habitat as opposed to in a zoo. While volunteering you might also get to experience first hand rhino conservation in South Africa, and take part in rhino dehorning.

Donate If you don’t have the ability to physically volunteer, make a difference and donate to the Wild Tomorrow Fund. Since they are a non-profit organization, your donations would directly impact the life of many animals, surrounding communities, and lastly saving habitats. Help make a difference and donate today!
